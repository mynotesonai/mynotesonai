Parametric Surfaces and Areas

Finding whether the points $ P(7, 10, 4) \ \ \text{and} \ \ Q(5, 22, 5) $ lie on the surface

$ r(u, v) = \begin{bmatrix} 2u+3v \\ 1+ 5u - v \\ 2 + u + v \end{bmatrix} $

$$ r(u, v) = \begin{bmatrix} 2u + 3v \\ 1+ 5u - v \\ 2 + u + v \end{bmatrix} \ \text{and} \ \text{can be written as} \ \ r(u, v) = \begin{bmatrix} (2u + 3v)i \\ (1+ 5u - v)j \\ (2 + u + v)k \end{bmatrix} $$

Finding the Parametric Equations of

$ x = 2u + 3v \\ y = 1 + 5u - v \\ z = 2 + u + v \\ $ These are the parametric equations that represents the surface. We will plug the point P into x and y

$ 7 = 2u + 3v \\ 10 = 1 + 5u - v \\ $ We can further simplify

$ 7 = 2u + 3v \\ 9 = 5u - v \\ $ We solve the simultaneous equation

$ 7 = 2u + 3v \\ +(27 = 15u - 3v) \\ $ By solving the simultaneous equation we get

$ u = 2 \ \text{and} \ v = 1 $

Now we plug u and v in the third equation $ z = 2 + u + v \text{ , } 4 = 2 + 2 + 1 $

If the point P lies on the surface represented by the vector equation, then when we plug-in Z value corresponding to the X and Y we already plugged in, we should get the same values for u and v. Plugging the values of u and v in the equation z = 2 + u + v we get
$ 4 = 2 + 2 + 1 $ Since $ 4 \neq 5 $ hence we can conclude that P does not lie on the surface.
