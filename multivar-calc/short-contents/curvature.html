Carvature

Curvature gives us the measure of how fast the direction changes as we move a small distance along the curve.

Curvature Formula
$ \Large{\kappa = || \frac{d\vec{T}}{ds}} $

Find the Curvature of the Curve $ \vec{r(t)} = \begin{bmatrix} 5t & \sin t & \cos t \end{bmatrix} $

$ \large{\kappa = \frac{|| \vec{r'(t)} \ \times \ \vec{r''(t)} ||}{|| \vec{r'(t)} ||^{3}}} $

$ \vec{r'(t)} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -\sin t, -\cos t \end{bmatrix} $

Compute cross product $\vec{r'(t)} \ \times \ \vec{r''(t)} = \begin{vmatrix} \vec{i} & \vec{j} & \vec{k} \\ 5 & \cos t & -\sin t \\ 0 & -\sin t & -\cos t \end{vmatrix} $

$ \vec{r'(t)} \ \times \ \vec{r''(t)} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 5\cos t & -5\sin t \end{bmatrix} $

Finding the magnitude, or length of $||\vec{r'(t)} \times \vec{r'(t)} || $

$ ||\vec{r'(t)} \times \vec{r'(t)} || = \sqrt{(-1)^{2} + (5\cos t)^{2} + (-5\cos t)^{2}} $

$ ||\vec{r'(t)} \times \vec{r'(t)} || = \sqrt{ 1 + 25 \cos^{2} t + 25 \sin^{2} t} $

$ ||\vec{r'(t)} \times \vec{r'(t)} || = \sqrt{26} $

calculating $ || \vec(t) ||^{3} $

$ ||\vec{r'(t)} ||^{3} = (\sqrt{(5)^{2} + (\cos t)^{2} + (-\sin t)^2})^{3} $

$ ||\vec{r'(t)} ||^{3} = (\sqrt{ 25 + \cos^{2} t + \sin^{2} t)^{3} $

$ ||\vec{r'(t)} ||^{3} = $

(\sqrt{ 25 + (\cos^{2} t + \sin^{2} t)})^{3} $ ||\vec{r'(t)}||^{3} = (\sqrt{ 25 + 1 })^{3} = (\sqrt{26})^{3} = 26^{\frac{3}{2}} $

$ Curvature for for $ \vec{r'(t)} is: $

$ \large{\kappa = \frac{\sqrt{26}}{26^{\frac{3}{2}}} = \frac{26^{\frac{1}{2}}}{26^{\frac{3}{2}}} = \frac{1}{26}}$
