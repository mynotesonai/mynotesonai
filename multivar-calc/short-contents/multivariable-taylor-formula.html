Multivariable Taylor Series

A Taylor Series or Taylor Polynomial is used to approximate another function by choosing a number both the function and the polynomial share. The primary objective is to take a hard function and turn into something easier.

Find the natural domain of $ f(x,y) = ln(x - y^{2})$

For $ ln(x - y^{2})$ to be defined, we need $ x - y^{2} > 0$ . Therefore the natural domain consists of all the points $ (x, y)$ that satisfy $ x - y^{2} > 0 $

Taylor Series is a specific kind of series of the form

$\sum_{n=0}^{\inf} \frac{f^{2}(a)}{n!} (x - a)^{n} = f(a) + \frac{f'(a)}{1!} (x - a) + \frac{f''(a)}{1!} (x - a)^{2!} + + \frac{f'''(a)}{3!} (x - a)^{3!} + .... $
