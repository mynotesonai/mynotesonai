Multivariable Calculus

Single variable calculus involves the study of functions of a Single variable, the concepts of Limits and Continuity, differentiation and integration. Second Order Partial Derivative Test

Use the second derivative test to find the global extrema $$ f(x.y) = 2x^2 + 3y^2 - 12x - 6y + 9 $$ $$ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 4x - 12 = 0; 4x = 12; x = 3 $$ $$ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 6y - 6 = 0; 6y = 6; y = 1 $$ $$ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 6y - 6 = 0; 6y = 6; y = 1 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2} = 4 $$ $$ \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2} = 6 $$ $$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2y} = 0$$ The second derivative test formula D = (Dxx)(Dyy) - (Dxy)^2 D = (4)(6)-(0)^2 D = 24 Second Derivative test if D is greater than 0 then (3, 1) is a saddle points
if D is equal to 0 then the result is inconclusive
if D s greater than 0 and Dxx is greater than 0 then (3, 1) is local minima
if D s greater than 0 and Dxx is less than 0 then (3, 1) is local maxima
In this scenario (3,1) is local minima
