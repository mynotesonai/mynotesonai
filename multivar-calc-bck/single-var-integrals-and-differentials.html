Single Variable Integrals and Differentials

Integration of the Polynomials

Given a polynomial of the form $ax^m$ ( a here is the constant coefficient and m is a costant exponent). Following are the rules of performing an indefinite integration operation over a single variable. Increase the exponent to m + 1 and then divide by m + 1

$$ \displaystyle \int ax^m \ dx $$ $$ \displaystyle \int ax^m \ dx = \frac {ax^{m+1}{b+1}} + C \ (if \ b \neq -1) $$ $$ $$ $$ \displaystyle \int ax^{-1} \ dx = \frac {a}{x} \ dx \ = a \ ln \ x + C $$
